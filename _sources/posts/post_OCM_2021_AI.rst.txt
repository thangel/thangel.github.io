Evaluating the eutrophication risk of an artificial tidal lagoon
-----------------------------------------------------------------

In a study published in *Ocean & Coastal Management*, we evaluate the eutrophication risk associated with an artificial tidal lagoon in Swansea Bay. The research assesses water quality impacts and concludes that the lagoon poses minimal risk to eutrophication under proposed operational modes.

**Abstract**

Tidal lagoons are proposed as a means of harnessing tidal range energy, but their environmental impacts, including effects on water quality, need to be assessed. This study evaluates the eutrophication risk of an artificial tidal lagoon in Swansea Bay, UK, using field measurements and numerical modelling. Water quality parameters, including nutrients, chlorophyll-a, and dissolved oxygen, were measured in the bay. A 2-D hydrodynamic model was used to simulate the operation of the lagoon under ebb-only and two-way modes. The results show that the lagoon has minimal impact on eutrophication risk, with no significant changes in nutrient concentrations or phytoplankton biomass. The study concludes that tidal lagoons can be designed to minimise environmental impacts, making them a viable option for renewable energy generation.
