Tidal range technology¶

Tidal range power plants exploit high water elevation differences naturally created in certain sites to drive flows through turbines, generating power in a predictable fashion. In recent years, there have been a number of scoping studies focused on harnessing the energy in areas with a large tidal range, driven in part by international commitments to satisfy energy demand from sustainable sources. Most recently, sites in the UK have been identified for their potential [Hen17], with detailed proposals for tidal range power plants in these areas having been put forward a number of times.
